## What Went Well

### 1. Empirical-verification pattern emerged at Epic 13 close

**Story 13-8 surfaced that Story 11-7's "25× reduction on user-derived tail" claim was empirically 6.7× — overstated by ~4×.** The discrepancy: Story 11-7 computed 25× against user-item CONTENT only (`12,000 / ~480 chars`); reality included `~1,318 chars` of `<USER_FACTS>` / `<USER_WEAK_AREAS>` wrapper text (bilingual "treat as data" prelude per Story 9-4 + section headers + tag delimiters) which dominate the post-truncation tail. This is the load-bearing demonstration that **verification stories produce real findings**, not just CI-gate ceremony. The 13-8 paragraph in CLAUDE.md now carries the corrected number with a clear discrepancy analysis.

### 2. Single-chokepoint-refactor + delete-don't-alias patterns carried forward consistently

Six of 8 Epic 13 stories used the Epic 12 "single chokepoint" discipline:
- **13-1** extracted `scheduleAiTextSetState` + `cancelPendingAiTextRaf` helpers as the ONE point of state mutation for `pendingAiText`.
- **13-2** consolidated 11 home-screen queries into ONE `get_home_aggregate` RPC.
- **13-3** consolidated 6 session-feedback queries into ONE `get_session_feedback_aggregate` RPC + ONE `useSessionFeedbackAggregate` hook.
- **13-4** extracted ONE `useMockTestGeneration` hook with internal `Promise.allSettled` parallelization.
- **13-5** replaced the screen's inline `<ScrollView><messages.map>` with ONE `<FlatList>` + ONE `Bubble` extracted component.
- **13-7** hoisted static styles to ONE module-level `ViewStyle` constant per touched component.

The "delete don't alias" rule fired in 13-1 (deleted `console.log` after calibration), 13-2 (deleted 8 legacy cache-invalidation writes), 13-3 (deleted 5 distinct Sentry tags consolidated to 1), 13-4 (deleted 220+ lines of for-loop + resume detection), 13-5 (deleted `messageLayoutMap` ref + per-row `onLayout` callback), 13-7 (deleted `className` from 3 animated wrappers). Each deletion was pinned by a drift detector negative-guard so the legacy pattern can't silently return.

### 3. Drift detector with comment-stripped source per Story 12-2 P12 became a project-wide idiom

Every Epic 13 story that touched source code added a drift detector test using the `stripComments` + scoped-regex pattern from Story 12-2 P12. Notable extensions:
- 13-1 + 13-4 + 13-5 + 13-7 all introduced *scoped extraction* helpers (`extractMethodBody`, `extractFunctionBody`, `extractOpeningTag`, `findTranscriptFlatListBody`) that anchor regexes to specific JSX elements / function bodies rather than file-wide. This systematically defeats the "file-wide false-positive" failure mode flagged in Story 13-2 P11.
- 13-7's `extractOpeningTag` walker added string-literal awareness (single/double/template + escape sequences) — applied Story 13-1 P3 lesson directly to JSX scope.

### 4. Calibration discipline ("range-based, not exact-value") replaced over-tight assertions

Story 13-7 review-round-1 P3 surfaced that `expect(...).toHaveLength(1)` on `react-test-renderer.findAll` results was too strict (multiple fiber-tree levels per logical component). The lesson — "use ranges + tolerance, not exact-value assertions" — was directly applied to Story 13-8's bound calibration (±2% tolerance + observed-value-floor/ceil pattern + 30% mode-spread tolerance vs spec's too-tight 10%).

### 5. Round-1 patch count stayed under 15 on every story

Epic 12's terminal action item proposed "round-2 auto-trigger when round-1 patches > 15". Every Epic 13 story stayed under: peak was 13-3 at 13 patches; average was 8.1. This is a substantial improvement over Epic 12's range (12-7 had 21, 12-8 had 19, 12-9 had 19). Possible drivers: smaller story scopes (verify-only 13-8 + drift-pin-only 13-6 + style-resolution 13-7 each kept diff < 350 lines), more mature drift-detector + test infrastructure, less migration work (only 13-2 + 13-3 added migrations vs Epic 12's many).

### 6. Audit accuracy held — every Epic 13 story closed its stated audit finding

P2-3 / P2-4 / P2-5 / P2-6 / P2-7 all closed cleanly. The two "P2-x performance" stories (13-6 Sentry + 13-7 className/style) closed audit-category findings that the audit didn't name specifically but that the roadmap explicitly carved out. No "stale audit finding" surprises like Story 12-10's pre-13-1 npm-audit reframing.

### 7. The "verify here from a perf POV" hint at Epic 13 line 255 produced the highest-value finding of the epic

The 13-8 spec was one sentence in the roadmap. The dev agent's calibration cycle (observe → set bounds → assert) ran into the 25× → 6.7× discrepancy purely by virtue of MEASURING what 11-7 had only ESTIMATED. **Measurement-as-a-first-class-deliverable** is the Epic 13 pattern most worth carrying forward.

### 4. Story 13-1 review-round-1 P1 was a "blindly simplified" regression caught by review

The Story 13-1 spec hint said "drop `extraData` on `TranscriptView` (or change to ref-stable)". The initial implementation removed it across both condensed + non-condensed branches. The Blind Hunter review caught that condensed mode legitimately needs the `${transcript.length}-${isAiSpeaking}` template — without it, side-note correction gating breaks. The fix was a **REVERT** in round-1: restore the condensed-branch ternary. The lesson — "spec hints can be wrong; verify against the actual code's downstream consumers" — became Story 13-7's review-round-1 P3 + Story 13-8's calibration tolerance. **But the root cause is a process question: should review-round-1 patches that REVERT round-0 implementation count differently from patches that just refine?** Story 13-1's 10 round-1 patches included 1 full revert + 9 refinements; treating those as the same severity inflates the "round-1 patches per story" metric.

### 5. Story 13-4 had ~30+ rejected adversarial-review findings — highest noise rate of the epic

The Edge Case Hunter + Blind Hunter on Story 13-4 surfaced ~55 findings total; ~30+ were rejected as noise (speculative future-SDK scenarios, false alarms, out-of-scope items). The signal-to-noise ratio on adversarial review is degrading as the codebase matures — most "what could go wrong" questions have been answered in prior stories, so new questions skew toward speculative. **Reviewers need a "what's NEW in this story" framing prompt, not just "be ruthless."** This was visible in the agents' own self-withdrawn findings count (~11 in Story 13-7's Blind Hunter run).

### 6. Per-story CLAUDE.md paragraphs grew to multi-page monsters

The Story 13-1 / 13-2 / 13-3 / 13-4 paragraphs each exceed 5,000 chars + multiple paragraphs of round-1 patch documentation. The CLAUDE.md is now ~80,000 chars total; the LLM context budget for ALL stories now sits at ~20,000 tokens just for the CLAUDE.md preamble. **The "story X invariant verified" link-back pattern compounds without bound.** Future stories should either (a) summarize prior stories in 1 sentence + link to the canonical paragraph, or (b) the retro should sweep and compress old paragraphs (Stories 9-X / 10-X are mature enough to compress).

### 7. Spec literalism vs reality tension surfaced multiple times

- Story 13-1: spec said "drop extraData"; reality needed conditional preservation in condensed mode.
- Story 13-7: spec said "use module-level constant"; runtime test discovered react-test-renderer surfaces multiple fiber-tree levels per logical Pressable, breaking `toHaveLength(1)` exact-cardinality.
- Story 13-8: spec said "Case 7 mode-invariance ±10% spread"; reality observed 23% (legitimate mode-block additions per Story 10-7).

The pattern: **spec writers under-specify edge cases that only surface at test-run time.** This is fixable by running the test ONCE with stub bounds during spec authoring (Story 13-8's calibration cycle made explicit). But it requires the spec author to actually run code, which the bmad-create-story workflow doesn't currently demand.

### 8. No PR review-and-merge cycle was used — all 8 PRs auto-merged after CI green

Every Epic 13 PR landed within hours of being opened with no human review beyond the dev agent + the 3-layer adversarial review. **This is a procedural risk that's only OK if the dev agent + adversarial review are reliable enough to substitute for human eyeballs.** The 65 round-1 patches across the epic suggest the adversarial review catches a LOT — but the 8-of-9-action-items-dropped pattern suggests humans aren't reading the retrospectives either. **For Epic 14 onward, a human merge-and-confirm gate before next-epic kickoff is probably worth adding.**

## Significant Discoveries

### 1. Story 11-7's "25× tail reduction" was empirically 6.7×

**This is the most important Epic 13 finding.** Pinned at CI via `MIN_REDUCTION_RATIO = 5` in Story 13-8. CLAUDE.md Story 11-7 paragraph **should be corrected**; the user-derived-tail reduction is ~85% (still meaningful), not 96% (the 25× figure implies).

### 2. Epic 13's ACs are met architecturally, not empirically

A user opening the app on a Pixel 4a still cannot be guaranteed ≥55 FPS during conversation, ≤1.5s home cold-cache, or ≤8s mock-test tap-to-playable. The ALGORITHMIC perf has been improved; whether the user-experienced perf matches is a Reactotron / Flipper question. **Epic 14 / 15 / 16 should NOT pretend Epic 13 closed those ACs without device verification.**

### 3. CLAUDE.md "perf claim drift" is structural

The Story 11-7 25× claim sat for 4 epics without anyone questioning it. Every per-story paragraph carries similar numerical claims (Story 11-2 144-288ms TTFT, Story 11-5 ~$0.001-$0.0018/session cost, Story 13-1 5-8 FPS drops, Story 13-7 50→55 FPS, Story 13-8 1.76¢/session). **Most are operator-estimated; none are CI-pinned.** Without device profiling, they're aspirational.

### 4. The bmad-create-story → bmad-dev-story → bmad-code-review → bmad-retrospective workflow is now mature and predictable

Across all 8 Epic 13 stories, the workflow produced consistent shapes: story file with calibrated AC + Dev Notes + invariants table → dev branch off main → ±5-15 round-1 patches → PR + done. No story stalled. The workflow itself is the most valuable artifact of Epic 12 + 13 combined.

### 5. Test-file pattern duplication is becoming structural debt

`MinimalTestInstance` local shim repeats in EmailVerificationGate.test.tsx (Story 12-9), animated-wrappers-render.test.tsx (Story 13-7), history-flatlist-virtualization.test.tsx (Story 13-5). `react-native-reanimated` jest.mock factory repeats verbatim in 3 files. `findAllNodes` / `flattenStyle` helpers repeat in 2 files. **These should be promoted to `src/test-utils/react-test-renderer.ts` + `src/test-utils/jest-mocks.ts` shared modules.** Epic 14 or 15.X should pick this up.

### Does Epic 13 require updating Epic 14's plan?

**No.** Epic 14 is UI/UX consistency — different agent, different surface (design tokens / icon system / language). The Epic 13 work hardened Stories 13-7's design-token discipline (`Colors.*` / `Radii.*` / `Shadows.*`), which directly supports Epic 14 deliverable 14.4 (token enforcement via ESLint). Epic 14's planned scope is sound.

1. **Verification stories produce real findings.** Story 13-8 surfaced that Story 11-7's "25× reduction" claim was empirically 6.7× — overstated by ~4×. The pattern "MEASURE what was previously ESTIMATED" is the highest-value discipline of Epic 13.

2. **Epic 13's ACs are architectural, not empirical.** No on-device profiling has happened. Before Epic 16 ships the app, the 3 ACs (iPhone 11 FPS, 4G first-paint, mock-test playable time) need real-device measurement. This is the load-bearing follow-up.

3. **Action items aren't getting done.** 8 of 9 Epic 12 AIs were open at Epic 13 close, repeating the Epic 10 → 11 pattern. Without a workflow change (block next-epic on AI status), retros are decorative.

4. **The Epic 11/12 patterns held perfectly:** single-chokepoint refactor + delete-don't-alias + drift detector + scoped extraction + range-based assertions. Six of 8 Epic 13 stories applied them cleanly. The discipline is now durable.

5. **Round-1 patch count stayed under 15 on every story.** This is a real improvement over Epic 12's worst case (12-7 at 21). Better story scoping (verify-only + drift-pin-only + small-refactor variants) drove it.

6. **CLAUDE.md is growing unboundedly.** ~80KB now; each new story adds a multi-thousand-char paragraph + a review-round-1 paragraph. Compression sweep is preventive maintenance (Action Item #9).

7. **The bmad-create-story → bmad-dev-story → bmad-code-review → bmad-retrospective workflow is mature.** Across 8 stories, predictable shapes + predictable patch counts + predictable test cases produced 8 clean PRs in rapid succession. The workflow itself may be the most valuable artifact of the project's last two epics.
