## What Went Well

1. **3-layer adversarial review is reliably catching real bugs.** Every Epic 14 story shipped only after R1 patches — averaging ~12 patches per story. Without these reviews, ~46 HIGH-severity correctness bugs would have shipped: Story 14-2's a11y regression on disabled `SkillCard`, Story 14-3's iOS-only `importantForAccessibility="no"` Icon decorative default, Story 14-5's 3 WCAG-AA contrast regressions, Story 14-7's routing race + speaking-unloadable bugs, Story 14-9's RN 0.74 `pointerEvents` deprecation. The review is the cheapest place to find these.
2. **Story 13-7's frozen-static-style + Shadows-spread-first pattern carried into 5 new components without breaking.** Pattern reuse is mechanical now. `Object.freeze({...Shadows.X, ...explicit-props}) as ViewStyle` exported `@internal` for runtime test pinning is the established discipline.
3. **Shared test utilities (Epic 13 AI #7) paid off immediately.** `src/test-utils/react-test-renderer.ts` (`MinimalTestInstance` + `findAllNodes` + `flattenStyle`) + `src/test-utils/mocks/reanimated.ts` factory were consumed by every Epic 14 runtime test — zero per-test boilerplate duplication.
4. **Drift detector discipline reached steady state.** Story 12-2 P12 (comment-stripped readFile) + Story 13-2 P11 (paired POSITIVE+NEGATIVE pins) + Story 13-7 R1-P4 (scoped-element extraction) + Story 14-2 R1-M7 (brace-exclusion JSX-comment stripping) form a copy-paste-tunable kit.
5. **Story 14-4's design-token gate caught real regressions in real time.** The bash + ESLint gate fired during Story 14-9 dev work, catching a pre-existing redundancy in `app/onboarding/index.tsx` that had been bypassing the original gate via `rounded-b-[28px]` side-specific syntax. The widened regex (Story 14-9 incidental fix) closed the loophole.
6. **Operator-decision items (Q1–Q5 per story) prevented mid-implementation pivots.** Every Epic 14 story resolved its 5 questions per Recommended without deviation. The Q-list format surfaces high-stakes choices BEFORE coding starts.
7. **Epic 13 retro AI #5 (adversarial review noise-reduction framing) is doing visible work.** Story 13-4 had 30+ rejected findings; Story 14-9 had ~14. The "anchor on what's NEW; self-withdraw on refute" framing improved signal-to-noise measurably.

## What Didn't Go Well

1. **Story 14-1 needed 15 R1 patches** — a structural drift-detector coverage gap allowed 8 user-visible bilingual regressions through (Profile StatTile labels, SKILL_LABELS.fr renders, mock-test render-flip, COMPAGNON uppercase, runtime greeting via `getGreeting()`, login Sign Up row, Settings 3 strings, CEFR-chart empty state). The global `FORBIDDEN_FR_SUBSTRINGS` allowlist started at 24 entries and ended at 41 after R1. **Lesson:** the FIRST story of an epic-wide rewrite needs an explicit "inventory completeness" gate; per-file pins miss what file-wide grep would have caught. Adding the global sweep was the structural fix.
2. **Story 14-4's 22 R1 patches** — the highest of the epic. Broad enforcement-rule changes (ESLint + bash gate + design-token migration) interact with every styled surface in the codebase. 5 visible-shadow regressions reverted; 6 enforcement-gap loopholes patched; magic-comment context-anchored; negative-numeric literals; path-based exempts; shadowOffset coverage. **Lesson:** when a story touches an enforcement layer, budget more review time than a standalone-feature story. The R1-patch baseline of ~12/story does NOT apply to gate-changing stories.
3. **5 of 9 Epic 13 retro AIs went UNADDRESSED in Epic 14.** Story 11-7 claim correction (#1 ✅), accountability gate (#4 ✅ via workflow), adversarial-prompt tuning (#5 ✅), bounded-budget pattern doc (#6 ✅ pre-existing), test-utils extraction (#7 ✅) were the wins. CLAUDE.md numerical-claim sweep (#2), device-profiling pass (#3 — Epic 16 prep), Epic 12 AI carry-forward (#8), CLAUDE.md compression (#9) all stayed open. **Lesson:** AIs that are orthogonal to the next epic's theme structurally don't get worked. The accountability gate surfaces them at kickoff but doesn't force resolution.
4. **CLAUDE.md grew, didn't shrink.** Per Epic 13 AI #9, mature Story 9-X / 10-X paragraphs should have been compressed. Instead, every Epic 14 story added a fresh long paragraph. CLAUDE.md is now ~90KB. The per-conversation context budget is eroding.
5. **Story 14-8 forgot to add a CLAUDE.md paragraph entirely.** Acceptance Auditor caught this during Story 14-9's review but flagged it as "not 14-9 scope". The Story 14-8 dev-story workflow didn't enforce the housekeeping step. **Lesson:** the dev-story completion checklist should include "CLAUDE.md paragraph added" as a non-skippable checkbox if the story file's File List includes CLAUDE.md.
